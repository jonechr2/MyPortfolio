Hi. I'm Chris.

First and foremost, I am a writer. It’s what I love to do. It’s how I conceptualize and articulate the world around me. But I’m also a tenacious problem solver and teacher. I'm a tinkerer and I like to get my hands dirty figuring out solutions. I believe that writing, at it's core, is problem solving. One of my favorite aspects of technical writing is getting to experience and learn new products and translate that to an end-user by creating dynamic and engaging learning experiences.

In my professional (and even personal) life, I am driven by four core tenets.

Service

I think it was Simon Sinek who said something to the effect of "We are not in charge of people, but responsible to take care of the people in our charge." The fundamental belief that leading is a privileged sacrifice, not a selfish move for personal power or gain is what informs how I lead, build and maintain relationships, and approach day-to-day work. My primary responsibility is to take care of those in my charge, even at person sacrifice. This truth has never been more evident that when the world was upended by the COVID-19 pandemic. Service to those in my charge meant showing an immense amount of empathy and willingness to accommodate as much as possible. And yes, this even meant driving around town trying to source toilet paper to send to the people on the team, or cooking meals to drop off for those working full time while keeping their children on track with school. None of this is required to manage, all is required to lead.

Passion

It’s cliché, but true, that I love technical writing and learning and development. As a tinkerer, figuring our strategies to help other learn provides me the chance to break down complex systems and explain them back to make people’s lives and jobs a little bit easier. I am dedicated to this task. I’ve also developed a passion for leading teams. I constantly strive to be better, knowing that as I progress in my career I won’t always get it right, but I’ll always learn and grow.

Problem Solving

I am a super sleuth. I have a desire to understand how and why things work. I use all available resources to solve problems and get the job done. This has been a major factor in my success as a technical writer, and as a leader of a tech writing and training team– I am always seeking understanding. Whether it’s interacting with SMEs, researching complex problems with team members, or working with clients to gather data, I endeavor to be a difference maker both as a leader and as an individual contributor.

In my technical writing position at Amazon, I was challenged from day one to help meet a critical deadline for launching a new training content platform. Between meetings with stakeholders, developers, and reworking legacy documentation with SMEs, I didn’t see my desk for my first two weeks on the job. Solving difficult technology problems in creative ways allowed me to make meaningful contributions from day one. As a result, we were able to successfully launch a logistics training platform in 11 languages to our employees all over the world. The experience of managing translation, developing content, and training team members on how to use the authoring tool helped to establish me as a leader.

Expertise

In my current leadership role at TEAM Software, I am a trusted expert in source control systems like Git, content management systems such as WordPress and Drupal, authoring tools such as MadCap Flare, and training tools like Captivate and Intellum’s Exceed LMS. I’m also the resident HTML, CSS, and JQuery expert, often helping our marketing department resolve issues with their WordPress website and email automations. I’m also a structured authoring and single sourcing expert, building web portals that leverage existing content but presenting them in dynamic and intuitive ways for our end users. Most recently, I built and deployed a sophisticated customer training and documentation portal (called the Learning Center) that combined all our documentation and training resources into a single place for our clients. This replaced the need to email large Word or PDF files back and forth to clients, while also allowing a single person to update multiple learning resources (course objectives, procedures, images) from a master source.

I’m also a recognized industry expert, contributing to technical writing, MadCap, and training groups via forums, Slack, conferences, and presenting webinars for MadCap on how to use relationship tables and build out dynamic learning experiences. I consistently strive to participate in professional development opportunities to better my leadership and technical skills.
